Story Behind the Research

From artifact intuition to public research infrastructure.

COSMOS-CQA began as a practical question: what would a careful public review surface look like if cosmology artifact QA had to be visible, reproducible, caveated, and inspectable from the start?

Origin concept

The early COSMOS work explored a citizen-assisted review idea: turn difficult visual residuals, sidecar signals, and quality labels into a workflow that people could inspect without pretending that the interface itself proved new science.

The core insight was not only visual review. It was evidence discipline. A useful research workbench should keep track of what was loaded, what was labeled, what was hidden from public reviewers, what hashes identify the artifacts, which reports were exported, and what caveats travel with every diagnostic placeholder.

Rework into a public research product

The current COSMOS-CQA public project converts the original browser prototype into maintainable research infrastructure: modular source, schema contracts, deterministic replay tests, Core Pack intake, evidence bundles, validation reports, SBOM references, and a public portal at the canonical domain.

The product stance is intentionally bounded. COSMOS-CQA is not a production decision system, not a scientific-claim engine, and not an OSI open-source release. It is a research-source workbench for artifact QA, provenance, reproducibility, and review.

Public science and data references

The concept is shaped by public cosmology data cultures around CMB maps, lensing products, survey catalogs, reproducible releases, and citizen science. COSMOS-CQA does not redistribute restricted third-party datasets; it uses synthetic examples, manifests, citations, and documented boundaries unless rights clearly permit more.

  1. ESA Planck Legacy Archive, public access point for Planck mission products used as a conceptual reference for CMB map and artifact-review workflows.
  2. Planck Collaboration, Planck 2018 results I, overview and cosmological legacy paper for the Planck 2018 release context.
  3. NASA LAMBDA WMAP data products, public WMAP mission data reference for CMB map-release and reproducibility expectations.
  4. NASA LAMBDA ACT products, public Atacama Cosmology Telescope data reference for modern CMB survey products and derived map workflows.
  5. Dark Energy Survey Data Release 2, public survey release reference relevant to catalog, imaging, and weak-lensing review cultures.
  6. Galaxy Zoo / Zooniverse, public citizen-science reference point for large-scale human review, classification, and research participation.

These references explain the public science context behind the workbench concept. They do not imply endorsement, data redistribution permission, scientific validation, or production fitness for COSMOS-CQA.

Current public research product

The current public product is a static, citable research portal plus browser workbench. It is designed to make the evidence trail visible: sample Core Pack loading, tile passports, labels, bookmarks, validation reports, evidence bundles, provenance hashes, and deterministic reload behavior.
