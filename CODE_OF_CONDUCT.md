# Code of Conduct

COSMOS-CQA is a research-source project for scientific and educational collaboration. Participants are expected to communicate respectfully, stay on topic, and help preserve a constructive research environment.

## Expected Behavior

Participants should:

- use welcoming, precise, and respectful language;
- focus criticism on ideas, evidence, code, data provenance, and claims;
- respect the COSMOS-CQA research-only license, contribution terms, and steward decisions;
- disclose relevant limitations, uncertainty, and data-use constraints;
- protect private, restricted, regulated, proprietary, or personally identifying information.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- harassment, threats, insults, abusive language, or discriminatory remarks;
- doxxing, exposure of private information, or pressure to disclose private affiliation details;
- spam, trolling, repeated off-topic posting, or disruption of project workflows;
- deliberate misinformation about project capabilities, validation status, or scientific claims;
- attempts to pressure contributors into violating data, license, institutional, legal, or regulatory restrictions;
- submitting credentials, secrets, restricted datasets, regulated data, or proprietary material without authorization.

## Reporting

Do not post private conduct concerns, security-sensitive details, secrets, or restricted data in public issues.

Report concerns privately to the repository steward through the official AI-Bio Synergy Holdings LLC contact channel. If a concern involves a vulnerability, credential, private data exposure, or restricted dataset exposure, use the repository security reporting process described in `SECURITY.md` when available.

Reports should include enough context for review, such as links, screenshots, timestamps, affected repository areas, and any immediate safety or data-handling concerns.

## Enforcement

AI-Bio Synergy Holdings LLC is the owner and steward of COSMOS-CQA. The steward may remove content, close issues, reject contributions, request edits, limit participation, block accounts, or take other repository actions needed to protect the project, contributors, license boundaries, and research integrity.

Enforcement decisions may consider severity, recurrence, intent, impact, and risk to contributors, data provenance, or public understanding of COSMOS-CQA.
